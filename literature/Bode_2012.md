Bode, Nikolai W. F., Daniel W. Franks, and A. Jamie Wood. 2012. “Leading from the Front? Social Networks in Navigating Groups.” _Behavioral Ecology and Sociobiology_ 66 (6): 835–43. [https://doi.org/10.1007/s00265-012-1331-6](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00265-012-1331-6).

Model based approach
- "navigation of collectively moving groups that includes preferential interactions between individuals as a way of imposing social network structures, known to be present in many species"
- "effective leadership can occur when leaders do not occupy frontal spatial positions ([position within group](position%20within%20group.md)) and when navigation tendency is appropriately balanced with social position"
- "small minorities can dominate movement decisions if they have navigational knowledge combined with influential social network positions"

Two states
1. Navigates by aligning with a target direction
2. Avoidance-Alignment-Attraction approach ([[Reynolds_1987]], [[Couzin_2002]], [[Couzin_2005]])
