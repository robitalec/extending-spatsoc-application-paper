Bottlenose dolphins

Vanguard position based on [position within group](position%20within%20group.md)

[information](information)

> Group [leader](leader.md) were defined as individuals who were in vanguard position (i.e., in front of other group members) for >20% of the time group was observed when travelling. Using vanguard position during group travel as a proxy for leadership is supported by the observation that individuals in the vanguard position are much more successful at changing group [direction](direction.md) than individuals not in this position. Consistent [leader](leader.md) were the small number of individuals that were identified as [leader](leader.md) within groups (using the above definition) significantly more often than expected based on chance (Lewis et al., 2011). [Leaders](leader.md) and [follower](follower.md) exist in all groups, but only a subset of individuals lead groups across sightings significantly more than expected (3 of 20 individuals tested, Lewis et al., 2011
