Herbert-Read, James E., Andrea Perna, Richard P. Mann, Timothy M. Schaerf, David J. T. Sumpter, and Ashley J. W. Ward. 2011. “Inferring the Rules of Interaction of Shoaling Fish.” _Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences_ 108 (46): 18726–31. [https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1109355108](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1109355108).

---

Data
- mosquitofish (_Gambusia holbrooki_)
- observations (video)

Methods
- [[speed]] = difference between XY / t
- [[direction]] = $atan2(y_{t} - y_{t-1}, x_{t} - x_{t-1})$
- [acceleration](acceleration.md) = difference in [[speed]]  / t
- [[turning speed]] = difference in [[direction]] / t

Results
- Three key rules for social interactions of mosquitofish
	1. Attraction forces are importance in maintaining [[group cohesion]]
	2. Repulsion is mediated principally by changes in speed
	3. Positions and directions of neighbours are correlated but individuals only respond to the single nearest neighbour
