Papageorgiou, Danai, and Damien R. Farine. 2020. “Shared Decision-Making Allows Subordinates to Lead When Dominants Monopolize Resources.” _Science Advances_ 6 (48): eaba5881. [https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aba5881](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aba5881).

---

[GPS](GPS.md) tagging helped authors understand vulturing guineafowl live in multilevel societies with stable groups, preferentially associated with specific other groups, each with many breeding males and females


Pairwise distances between all  groups members when the group was cohesive

Cohesive defined as all 5 minute intervals where no pairwise distance exceeded 100 m  

When cohesive, group's position was calculated as the group's centroid

[Direction](direction.md) determined by watching animations of [GPS](GPS.md) data

Initiators defined as individuals that moved more than 20 m away from the center of the group. Departure order of individuals. Initiation successful if all group members followed the [direction](direction.md) of the initiator and unsuccessful if not all followed or if the initiator returned to the group. 

> To determine whether the departures by the last individual on  each patch are caused by depletion or not, we test whether these  individuals express disproportionate movement characteristics that indicate that they are being pulled away from the patch. We use data  from a group where all members were fitted with Global Positioning System ([GPS](GPS.md)) tags to calculate the distance from the last individuals at the moment of leaving the patch to the rest of the group and their [speed](speed.md) while rejoining the group.


Distance and [speed](speed.md) in rejoining group for individuals at the back of a group

Compared to group's centroid ([position within group](position%20within%20group.md))

Solar-powered battery
